In-Depth Studies

In-depth studies involve students carrying out individual research on topics decided upon with the Course Director:

  • Of a compulsory or elective provided for in the program structure and passed
  • Of a PhD course entered in the study plan

Student evaluation is based on a written assignment for each in-depth study, which is marked out of 30.

One in-depth study can be allocated to each course, worth a maximum of 3 credit points. Thus, ONLY one elective can be replaced by 2 in-depth studies, keeping other requirements in mind.

To have an in-depth study recorded in their study plan, students must contact the secretary's office of the Department of reference of the course chosen.

Higher Level Courses (PhD)

Some highly-motivated and deserving students will be allowed to obtain academic credit from "higher level courses", i.e. courses held as part of the following Bocconi PhD Programs:

  • PhD in Economics and Finance
  • PhD in Business Administration and Management
  • PhD in Social and Political Science
  • PhD in Statistics and Computer Science

Students enrolled in ESS programs can choose up to 3 PhD courses. Of these, they can request recognition of 2 courses in place of MSc electives and 1 course as an additional course.

Selection Requirements:
  • Graduation grade of undergraduate program of at least 105/110
  • Students enrolled in the first or second year (as regular students 'in corso') for 2025-2026
  • Applications from "reiscritti" and/or "fuori corso" will not be considered
  • Cannot apply for PhD courses in the same semester as Exchange or Free-mover programs
  • Students admitted to a Double Degree program are excluded
Selection Criteria:
  • Percentage of credit points earned (by end of February exam session)
  • GPA (weighted on credit points)
  • Letter of motivation
  • CV
  • Any GMAT or GRE certification
  • Graduation grade of undergraduate degree

Enhancing Experiences

Individual program structures include 2 compulsory credit points dedicated to curricular supplementary activities in the second year.

Activities may be completed in advance during the first year if spots are available. Examples include:

  • IT courses (IT Education Center)
  • Introductory Language courses (Language Center)
  • Course on thesis writing
  • Workshops in collaboration with ISPI
  • Broaden Your Frame Seminars
  • Media Experience
  • Skills and self-empowerment courses
